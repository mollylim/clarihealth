Our mission

Health information that truly reaches everyone

Every woman deserves to fully understand her healthcare options — regardless of her language, cultural background, or where she accesses care. Clari Health is being built to make that a reality.

Clear, animated videos and infographic guides for women's health procedures

Our content explains the purpose, benefits, and risks of common procedures and treatments — with warmth, clarity, and cultural sensitivity built in from the start.

Videos are designed for use by clinicians in any clinical or community setting, while patients can access infographics and educational content at home — in their own language, in their own time.

Why this matters

Addressing a critical gap in women's healthcare

8.4 yrs
life expectancy gap at birth for women in the most deprived areas compared with the most affluent in England
2×+
babies born to Black women are over twice as likely to die in their first year compared with those born to white women — Black women also experience the highest maternal mortality rates
1 in 3
births in England and Wales are now to women born outside the UK — rising to nearly 3 in 5 in London (ONS, 2023)
9%
only ~9% of births in responding NHS Trusts were recorded as requiring an interpreter — yet an estimated 37,000 mothers per year may struggle to communicate without support (MacLellan et al., 2024)

The NHS faces persistent challenges: language barriers, inconsistent interpreting services, and variable consent standards. No current tool addresses the full breadth of women's health needs or the specific requirements of ethnic minority patients.

Clari Health is being built to directly address these inequalities — combining the proven power of animation with multilingual content and cultural nuance into a single, scalable system.

  • Improves safety outcomes for ethnic minority women
  • Reduces inconsistency in consent quality across NHS trusts
  • Reduces clinical time spent on basic procedure explanations
  • Supports patients to participate meaningfully in their own care
